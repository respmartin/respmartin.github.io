About

I grew up in Granada, in the south of Spain — a city of medieval palaces, flamenco echoing off stone walls, and, most importantly, Real Madrid on TV every weekend. From an early age I was drawn to how systems work: radio signals, antennas, the invisible architecture beneath modern communication. That curiosity carried me through a BS and MS in Telecommunications Engineering at the Universidad de Granada, then — after winning the La Caixa fellowship, one of Spain’s most prestigious academic grants — across the Atlantic to UC San Diego for a second MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering.

I have spent 15+ years building and verifying the scientific software that engineers trust when lives and missions are on the line. At Delcross Technologies in Chicago, I helped develop Savant — the engine behind HFSS SBR+, Ansys’s industry-leading electromagnetic ray-tracing solver. At Ansys, I grew from engineer to technical leader, owning the verification strategy for HFSS: defining quality standards, building testing infrastructure, and making sure one of the world’s most complex physics simulators does exactly what it promises. Since 2017, I have called the San Francisco Bay Area home, and in 2025 I joined Synopsys — the largest EDA company in the world — as a Sr. Staff Engineer in Verification Engineering.

Outside the terminal, I pick up my guitar, lace up for a run through the hills, lose myself in a novel, or try out a new recipe from whatever corner of the world is currently calling to me. I still catch every Real Madrid match I can. Some things do not change.
